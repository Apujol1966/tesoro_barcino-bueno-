Life of a Champion

The First Olympic Champion of Barcino

Born on the ides of February in the year 96 A.D. in Barcino, Lucius achieved eternal glory in the year 129 A.D. during the 227th Olympiad. He won the chariot race with a mastery that earned him the first Olympic laurel for a citizen of Barcelona.

His victory was so legendary that he distributed 33 honorary plaques across Europe. Today, his honor lives on: the Generalitat awards the 'Luci Minici Medal' to Catalan Olympic athletes, and his name presides over the main promenade of the Montjuïc Olympic Ring.

A Life in Service to the Empire

Lucius performed an astonishing political and military career (*cursus honorum*). He served in the legions of Dacia, Moesia, and Pannonia, and was Legate of the Legio VI Victrix in Britannia. He was Consul of Rome and finally Proconsul of Africa, the position from which he wrote the letter you deciphered today. He was also appointed Augur, one of the highest religious dignities of the Empire.

The Great Patron of Barcino

In the year 125 A.D., he donated spectacular Public Baths to Barcino, built on his own land. Today we know that those baths lie beneath the foundations of the current Plaça de Sant Miquel. His love for Barcino was such that he bequeathed a fortune to ensure annual donations to his fellow citizens on the anniversary of his birth.
