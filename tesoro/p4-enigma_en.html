The Strength of Barcino

You have reached the place of rest.

🏛️ City Archives:

Below this square were the Baths of Barcino, the great social center of the colony. They had hot and cold baths, and were a gift from my own family for the enjoyment of all citizens.

"I have arrived where once steam clouded the sight and water healed the muscles after exhaustion. Today, instead of my baths, you will see a network of iron threads that intertwine seeking the sky.

It is a monument to the Castellers, men and women who, with a balance and strength that remind me of my years in Olympia, raise human towers defying the gods. Look for the name of the one who shaped this metal at its base.

Who is the author of this sculpture?"

That is not the name I seek. Look at the plaque on the base of the monument.
Hint: Approach the sculpture. On the metal plate on the ground or on one side of the base, the name of the artist who designed it appears. His name starts with Antoni.
