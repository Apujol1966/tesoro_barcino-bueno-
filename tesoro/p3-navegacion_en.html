The Order of the Chariots

"As you walk down the stones of the narrow Baixada de Santa Clara, to your left will rise the walls that keep the memory of the kings.

Before entering the courtyard of the kings, stop at the corner and look under the sign that names the street. As an expert charioteer, I am fascinated to see how citizens of other centuries also had to impose order on their carts and beasts so as not to collapse these alleys.

There, a ceramic plaque indicates the mandatory path for horses and points to the seat of a great royal archive.

Observe the ceramic on the corner. According to the label accompanying the drawing of the horses, to which Crown does this archive belong?"

That is not the correct name. Look at the ceramic right below the street name.
Hint: On the wall of the building on the left, just before entering the square, there is a tile with a drawing of a horse. The text says "Arxiu de la Corona d'Aragó".
